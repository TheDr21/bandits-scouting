Pitch approach
Don't give in — pitch around her with runners on, especially behind Maryanna and Tenley. She doesn't chase much and makes consistent hard contact, so middle-of-the-plate mistakes get punished. Khloe sprays line drives to left and center, where she's nearly automatic (.882 on balls to LF, .793 to CF), but a huge share of her outs come at shortstop — 59 of her 116 in-play outs were fielded by the SS. She's a left-center, line-drive hitter who beats you in the gaps; the ground balls she can't keep off the left-side infield are where she makes outs. Position your shortstop deep and toward the hole, and don't let outfielders play her to right.

Defensive positioning vs Khloe
Shortstop is the key. 69 balls went to the SS area — by far the most of any zone — and 59 were outs. Play your SS deep and shaded toward the 5-6 hole; that's where her hard ground balls get converted.
Don't cheat the outfield to right. She's a dead left-center hitter in the air — LF and CF combined for 53 hits and most of her extra-base damage. Keep your LF and CF honest and deep; the gap is where her doubles and triples land.
Right field is a low-volume escape. Only 28 balls went oppo and she hit just .474 there. Pitching her in (to push contact the other way) trades power for manageable contact.
10 triples tells you she runs well and finds the gaps — outfield communication and quick relays matter, don't let a gapper become a triple.
