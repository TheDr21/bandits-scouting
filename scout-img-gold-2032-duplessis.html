⚠ Top-heavy lineup — a sharp top five, then a steep drop-off
This team's offense lives at the top: Lanah Y (.464), Sydney P (.444), Riley D (.373), Bernie B (.367), and Ruby S (.362) are all real threats, but the bottom of the order falls off a cliff — Faith D (.233), Adalyn E (.223), Kailey S (.140), and Layla B (.129) are clear pressure points. If you navigate the top five, the back third of the order is where you take control. Lanah Y is the engine both ways: best bat on the team and the most-used arm.
Team identity
Top five carry the offense. Five hitters at .360+; the rest of the qualified lineup is .291 or below. Win the matchups against the top of the order.
Ruby S is the table-setter. Team-high 71 runs and 149 AB — she's the leadoff engine who turns the lineup over. Keep her off base.
Pitching is hittable and walks people. Every primary arm sits 3.63–4.65 ERA with a 1.64+ WHIP and high walk totals (51, 68, 37 BB). Be patient — free passes and base traffic are available.
Strikeout-prone bottom half. Vivian G (29% K), Mayci M (24%), Adalyn E (20%) — go right after the lower-order bats.

Approach
Respect the top five, attack the bottom four. Pitch around Lanah Y and Sydney P with runners on; make Riley/Bernie/Ruby earn it but don't walk into trouble. From the 8-9 holes down, challenge in the zone — those hitters strike out and rarely walk. On offense, be patient against their staff: all three primary arms walk a lot and give up hard contact, so work counts and let them beat themselves.

Read on the staff
Be patient — this whole staff walks people. 51, 68, and 37 walks from the three primary arms. Working counts and laying off borderline pitches will draw free passes and run up pitch counts.
Lanah Y is the workhorse (97 IP) and pitches when it matters, but she's hittable — 1.70 WHIP, 114 hits allowed. She's their best arm mostly by volume and competitiveness, not dominance.
Polly C is the most vulnerable. 4.65 ERA, 1.84 WHIP, and a team-high 68 walks. If she's in the circle, be selective early and make her throw strikes — base traffic is there for the taking.
No shutdown ace. All three sit 7.4–8.2 K/9 with high WHIPs. Put the ball in play, force them to field, and capitalize on the walks. This staff can be scored on.
